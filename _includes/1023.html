The professional body that accredits your course has different requirements to that of the University. Accreditation rules for your degree allow a maximum of 30 soft-failed credits across the whole degree.

What this means for progressing students:

Whilst you have met the University’s Progression Requirements to progress onto the next stage of your course, we strongly recommend that you retake your failed modules from this year to keep on track for an accredited degree.

What this means for graduating students:

There are further steps needed in order for you to meet the Progression Requirements of your course.

In order to graduate with accreditation, you must retake, and pass, any failed assessments during the upcoming Resit Examination period.
