Progression

There are further steps needed in order for you to meet the Progression Requirements of your course. You are currently unable to progress and must retake part or all of the academic year.

Due to extenuating circumstances, attempts will be treated as first sits. This means that you will be permitted to attend to take all modules again for the relevant stage and when you do, it will be as if you are sitting them for the first time.

In line with the EC regulations, the mark for your original attempt will be zero.

You must retake and pass this stage to progress on your course.

Please check for any required actions below.

Next steps

Deferral of Reassessment

  • To opt to defer your reassessments to the next academic year, complete this form
  • Forms submitted in time will be responded to within 2 weeks of the deadline.
  • Submission does not guarantee approval, you must wait for the official outcome.
  • Important - you will extend the length of your course by a year if you defer your reassessments.

Resit Fees

  • If you have one or more resits, a resit fee is required and payable in the NottinghamHub
  • The fee will be showing two weeks before, and for the duration of, the reassessment period
  • If the fee is unpaid after resits are taken, the fee will be added to your University account
  • There is no charge for first sits

Check outstanding debts

  • If you have any outstanding debt, your degree will not be conferred and you cannot attend a graduation ceremony
  • Check your status here: Student Fees and Payments
  • Or contact the Finance Office

Your contact information

  • Keep your contact information up to date through the NottinghamHub

Check Moodle and Exam Information

  • Use Moodle to access module content for revision and to resubmit any coursework
  • Resit exam information including dates, timetables and fees Assessment and Examinations

Returning to Study

  • Returning from a period of interruption? A ‘return to study’ form will be sent to your email.
  • Your record will be updated ready for your return.

Registering for the next academic session

  • Subject to satisfying progression requirements, you will need to register for the next academic session

Module Enrolment

  • If you have registered online, you can access module enrolment to choose or change your modules if required Module Enrolment Information

Support

Understanding your results

  • If you need any help understanding this outcome, please contact your Personal Tutor or Here For You Guides in the first instance
  • If unavailable, please use the Student Enquiry Centre

Academic Appeals

  • You have 30 days to submit an appeal from the date of this message
  • Appeals are only considered if you have grounds and provide supporting evidence
  • For further information, please see Academic Appeals
  • If an appeal is ongoing, you should proceed as if the original decision stands

Extenuating Circumstances

  • If you have not previously submitted an EC, you must follow the Academic Appeals procedure
  • You will need to provide new evidence that was not, and could not have been, made available to the Board of Examiners at the time of its original decision.

Students’ Union support

  • SU Education Advisers can help advise on appeals or EC late claims SU Advice

Reassessment/First Sit Marks

  • If you have taken a reassessment/first sit not in attendance this year, the mark will show against your module from a previous year.
  • If you have taken a reassessment/first sit in attendance this year, you may see the mark appearing in this year’s record and against your module from a previous year.
